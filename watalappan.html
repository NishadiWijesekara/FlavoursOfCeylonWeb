Sri Lankan Watalappan Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup palm sugar (jaggery), grated
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts, chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a saucepan, heat coconut milk and grated palm sugar over medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely. Let it cool slightly.
  3. In a mixing bowl, beat eggs well.
  4. Add cardamom powder, nutmeg powder, and vanilla extract to the beaten eggs. Mix well.
  5. Slowly pour the warm coconut milk mixture into the beaten eggs while continuously whisking to prevent curdling.
  6. Strain the mixture to remove any lumps.
  7. Divide the mixture into individual ramekins or a large baking dish.
  8. Place the ramekins or baking dish in a larger baking pan. Pour hot water into the larger pan to create a water bath (bain-marie).
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes or until the watalappan is set but still slightly wobbly in the center.
  10. Remove from the oven and let it cool completely.
  11. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.
  12. Garnish with chopped cashew nuts and raisins before serving, if desired.

Enjoy your Sri Lankan Watalappan!
